Conclusion:
i. Buy USDT on Binance using your physical bank account (whether in EGP or USD, you have both).
ii. Sell this USDT for someone who will deposit money on your electronic bank (whatever this bank is. Maybe skrill, elevate, I still haven't read about
them).
iii. Send the money from your electronic bank to interactive brokers (or whatever broker).
iv. Now to withdraw your money, send it from interactive brokers to your electronic bank.
v. On Binance, offer to fund someone's wallet in an electronic bank for USDT.
vi. Sell the USDT for cash in your physical bank account.
Buy process: physical bank --> USDT --> electronic bank --> broker
Sell process: broker --> electronic bank --> USDT --> physical bank
Questions:
a. Why don't we move between physical bank and broker directly? For 2 reasons:
	= Some people say that your physical bank may not accept to send or receive money from a broker.
	= It seems like higher fees (haven't yet known the fees in the P2P process, but it is $25/trade for HSBC international transfer).
b. Why don't we remove Binance (i.e. USDT) from the whole process?
	= Don't really know, but I think the problem will be in the fees from transferring from my physical bank to the electronic bank because I
	believe this will be an international transfer.
c. Why don't we remove the electronic bank from the whole process?
	= Because IBKR doesn't accept cryptocurrencies transfers directly.
