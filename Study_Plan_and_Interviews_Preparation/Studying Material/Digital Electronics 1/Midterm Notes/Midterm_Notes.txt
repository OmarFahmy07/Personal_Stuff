When a gate size is increased, its resistance decreases and capacitance increases. Thus, its intrinsic delay does not change,
while its extrinsic delay decreases. Thus, when a gate size is increased, its propagation delay decreases. However, the load
on the previous gate increases and hence its delay increases.

When a gate is scaled, its intrinsic delay does not change.

Steps to calculate the normalized intrinsic delay (p) of any gate:
1) Size it such that it has pull-up & pull-down resistances = Ro.
2) Count the number of caps at the output node (i.e. drain capacitance).
3) Divide by 3.

Steps to calculate the logical effort (g) of any gate:
1) Size it such that it has pull-up & pull-down resistances = Ro.
2) Count the number of caps at the input (i.e. gate capacitance).
3) Divide by 3.

Disadvantages of static CMOS logic family compared to ratioed logic families:
1) Area: due to the PUN that contains PMOS transistors which we size large to overcome their less mobility compared to NMOS.
2) Delay: due to the PUN that contains large PMOS transistors and hence large caps at the output node. Also, the output of a
gate is applied to the input of both PUN & PDN of the next gate, hence it sees larger loading and hence more delay.
